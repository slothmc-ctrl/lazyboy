🦥 Install Lazyboy

Your AI sidekick for Chrome — a 3-minute setup

Step 1

Open Chrome's hidden Extensions page

Type this in your address bar and hit Enter:

chrome://extensions/

This is a special Chrome page — it only works in Chrome!

Step 2

Flip the "Developer mode" switch

Look in the top-right corner of the page. Find this toggle and turn it ON:

Developer mode  

This unlocks the "Load unpacked" button we need next.

Step 3

Click "Load unpacked" and pick the folder

Three new buttons appear at the top. Click the one that says "Load unpacked", then navigate to this folder:

/home/slothmc-ctrl/lazyboy/dist-chrome

Select it and click "Open" (or just press Enter).

Psst! Your dev server is already running! Any code changes will auto-rebuild here.
Step 4

Grant extra permissions (important!)

Find the lazyboy card on the extensions page and click "Details".

Scroll down and turn these ON:

  • Allow access to file URLs — lets lazyboy read local files
  • Allow user scripts — lets lazyboy interact with pages (if shown)
Step 5

Pin it to your toolbar

Click the puzzle piece 🧩 icon next to your address bar.

Find lazyboy in the list and click the 📌 pin icon.

Now you'll see the lazyboy icon in your toolbar at all times.

Step 6

Open it!

You can open lazyboy two ways:

Method A: Click the lazyboy icon in your toolbar
Method B: Press Ctrl + Shift + S

A side panel slides out from the right — that's lazyboy! 🎉

🦥 You're all set!

lazyboy is now live in your Chrome. Try asking it to summarize a page, take a screenshot, or analyze anything on the web.

Troubleshooting? Click here

"Manifest is missing or unreadable" → Make sure you picked the dist-chrome folder (not lazyboy itself).

Side panel doesn't open → Click the puzzle 🧩, find lazyboy, right-click → "Open side panel".

Extension errors → On chrome://extensions/, click "Errors" on the lazyboy card. Or reload it with the ↻ refresh button.
